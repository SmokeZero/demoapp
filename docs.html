Getting Started

Welcome to the Open Traffic AI Lab documentation! This guide will help you set up your environment and make your first submission.

1. Account Creation

First, create an account on our platform. This will allow you to track your progress, submit models, and interact with the community.

2. Choose a Challenge

Browse the Challenges page to find an active challenge that aligns with your interests and skills. Each challenge page provides a detailed description, dataset links, and evaluation criteria.

3. Download Starter Kit

For each challenge, we provide a starter kit, typically a Colab-ready Jupyter notebook, that includes baseline code, data loading examples, and a basic model implementation. This helps you quickly get started.

4. Develop Your Model

Train your AI model using the provided dataset. You can use any framework (TensorFlow, PyTorch, etc.) and develop locally or on cloud platforms.

5. Make Your First Submission

Once your model is trained, follow the model submission guidelines to upload your model or containerized code. Our system will automatically evaluate your submission.

Challenge Guidelines

To ensure fair play and a productive environment, please adhere to our challenge guidelines:

  • All submissions must be original work.
  • Refer to specific challenge pages for detailed rules and permissible external resources.
  • Collaboration is encouraged, but team submissions must be clearly declared.
  • Any attempt to manipulate the leaderboard or evaluation system will result in disqualification.

Data Formats

Our datasets are provided in standard formats for ease of use. Common formats include:

  • Images: PNG, JPEG
  • Sensor Data: CSV, ROS bag files (for simulation data)
  • Annotations: JSON, XML (COCO format for object detection)

Detailed schema and examples are available in each challenge's starter kit.

Model Submission

Models can be submitted in two primary ways:

  1. Trained Model Files: Upload your trained model weights (e.g., .h5, .pth, .onnx) along with a prediction script.
  2. Containerized Code: For more complex models or custom environments, you can submit a Docker image containing your code and dependencies.

Refer to the specific challenge's submission section for precise instructions and file size limits.

Evaluation Metrics

Model performance is typically evaluated based on a combination of accuracy and inference speed. Common metrics include:

  • Accuracy: Mean Average Precision (mAP), F1-score, IoU (Intersection over Union)
  • Speed: Inference time per frame/sample (milliseconds)

The exact weighting and primary metric for ranking will be specified on each challenge page.

Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions here. If you can't find what you're looking for, please visit our Community Forum.

Q: Can I use external datasets?

A: Unless explicitly stated otherwise on the challenge page, only the provided datasets are allowed for training to ensure a fair competition.

Q: What programming languages are supported?

A: Our platform supports models developed in any language that can be containerized (e.g., Python, C++). Python with popular AI frameworks (TensorFlow, PyTorch) is most common.

Q: How often is the leaderboard updated?

A: The leaderboard updates in real-time as new submissions are evaluated. You'll see your rank change almost instantly.
